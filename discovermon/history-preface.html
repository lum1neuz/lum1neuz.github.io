Primitive Life on Mongolian Soil

Man first inhabited the territory of present-day Mongolia in the 500-700th millennium BC. Archaeologists have identified a distinct Mongolian people as early as the second millennium BC. Several states of Mongolian tribes, such as the Hunnu, Jujan, Turkic, Uigur and Kidan, succeeded each other before the 12th century AD.
