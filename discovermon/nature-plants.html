Plants

Mongolia's diverse and distinctive vegetation includes an important part of Asia's plant life. Species representative of Siberia's coniferous taiga forest, Central Asia's steppe and desert, and the Altai and Sayan mountains all occur here. Steppe plants from Kazakhstan grow beside Manchurian steppe flowers. Over 100 plant species are listed in the Mongolian Red Book as rare or endangered.

Flora and Plant Resource of Mongolia

According to study work of Mongolian flora, our scientists have established 2823 species of high vascular plants in 662 general of 128 families and 920 species of lichen, 417 species of moss, 520 species of fungus, and 728 species of algae. And we have a herbarium which has more than a 100 of thou-sands of a collection, which is registered with the world foundation of herbarium.
The largest families of flowering plants in Mongolia are-Asteraceae (407 species), Fabaceae (312), Poaceae (250), Rosaceae (140), Brassiceae (135), Cyperaceae (127), Ranunculaceae (119), Chenopodiaceae (90), Lamiaceae (86), Caryophyllaceae (83), Scrophulariaceae (77), Polygonaceae (67), Apiaceae (66), Salicaceae (50), Liliaceae (49). The largest genera of the flora of the country are-Astragalus (11 species), Artemisia (105), Oxytropis (94), Carex (86), Potentilla (61), Saussurea (49), Afflum (48), Salix (44), Taraxacum (37),
