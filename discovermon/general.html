General Information

Official Name: Mongol Uls

Territory: 1,565,000 sq km

Population: 3,122,523 (2016 December 1st)

Density: 1.99 person per sq km

Climate: Continental, warm rainy summers and extremely cold winters. Sharp seasonal fluctuations and variations. Great diurnal temperature changes.

Terrain: Mountains (40% of the territory) and rolling plateaus with vast semi desert zone in the south (Gobi).
Average altitude: 1580 m
Highest peak (in the Altai): 4374 m
Lowest point (in the east): 532 m

Regions: 21 aimags (provinces)

Capital: Ulaanbaatar
Population: 1,345,000

Economy: Traditionally based on breeding livestock and agriculture, mining (copper, gold, coal and others)

Total livestock population: 51.98 million (as of the closing result of 2014)
Sheep: 23214 thousand
Goats: 22008 thousand
Cattle: 3413 thousand
Horses: 2995 thousand
Camels: 349 thousand

Religions: Buddhism 94%, Muslim, Shamanism and Christian

Language: Mongolian

Most spoken foreign languages: Russian, English, German, Japanese and Korean.

Time: +8 hours to GMT.
+1 daylight saving time.

Currency: Tugrik (MNT)
Meronym: Mungu. But these coins are not in circulation as currency any longer, as their value is hundredth fraction of one tugrik.

No limit on foreign currency. Exchanging bureaus are at most hotels.
Major foreign currencies, credit cards, and traveller's cheques are accepted (mainly American Express, Visa, JCB, Master Card, and Diners Club).

Electric current: The standard voltage is 220 volts/50Hz. Sockets require a two-pin plug or an adapter.

Communications: International telephones are available in hotels. All networks are operated by Mobicom, Unitel, Skytel and G-Mobile. The network covers the capital and all the biggest towns of provinces. Mobicom also offers the Roaming service. But it's suggested to check the comptability and agreement on Roaming with your home service operator and Mobicom. Available rent of satellite mobile phones. Internet access is available in Ulaanbaatar at business centres (often located in hotels), Internet cafes and at the telephone exchange on province centres. ISPs include Univision, Skymedia, Railcom Bodicomputers, MagicNet, Mobinet, Erdemnet, Sky C&C, Citinet and Micom etc.

Food: Mongolian food is beef, mutton, dairy products, pasta, rice and seasonal fruits and vegetable. A variety of restaurants in Ulaanbaatar offer traditional Mongolian food as well as European and Asian cuisine.
